Study: Wearable camera reduces collision risk in blind, visually impaired

See full article here

Study done by JAMA Opthalmology developed a vision device designed to assist the blind and visually impared. Its goal is to reduce the number of accidents and collisions that happen in a daily basis for these groups

By wearing the device and assisted with mobility aids (A long cane or a guide dog), it reduces the risk of collisions and falls by nearly 40% by analysing movements and objects in close proximity to the person.

As Luo (an associate professor ofophthalmology at Harvard Medical School in Cambridge, Mass states:

"Many blind individuals use long canes to detect obstacles [and] collision risks are not completely eliminated. We sought to develop ... a device that can augment these everyday mobility aids, further improving their safety.
